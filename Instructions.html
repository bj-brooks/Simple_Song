Simple Song - Instructions


1. You are to write a simple song with piano accompaniment.


2. The work may be written for the voice range of your choice.  Whichever voice range you choose, make sure to keep within the ranges used in four voice part writing.


3. USE CORRECT PART-WRITING:


  1. • Root position and first inversion chords may be used freely.

  2. • Second inversion chords may be used, but only as long as they fall into one of the four categories studied (cadential, passing, arpeggiated and stationary).  At least ONE  second inversion chord MUST  be used.

  3. • Non-chord tones MUST  be used.  You are required to use at least one each of the following: (1) passing tone; (2) suspension; (3) neighbor group; (4) appoggiatura; (5) neighbor tone; (6) anticipation.  More than  one each may be used! The non-chord tones need NOT  be only in the voice part, feel free to use them in the accompaniment too.

  4. • Dominant Seventh Chords are allowed.  You are required  to use at least one dominant seventh chord in your composition (any inversion is acceptable).

  5. • NO parallel octaves, fifths or unisons!  Also, no consecutive octaves or fifths are allowed. CHECK CAREFULLY - especially between voice and accompaniment.

  6. • Avoid the melodic augmented second and fourth   (diminished fifth) at all costs.

  7. • Only major or minor keys may be used (NO MODES).


4. Your song will be four PHRASES long. The form of your song will be most likely be two periods that are parallel, contrasting or a mixture of the two (a, a’, b, b’), (a, b, c, c’), (a, a’, b, c), (a, b, c, d). It could, depending on your cadence structure, be a double period.


5. The composition should consist of a simple melody (vocal part) with a THREE voice piano accompaniment (one note in the bass  - left hand, and two notes in the treble - right hand) which is written in a rather slow harmonic rhythm. It is possible that the piano part may consist of FOUR voices, but ONLY when there is a rest in the voice part (one note in the bass and three in the upper part).


6. The text for the voice part may  ONLY  be selected from those given with these instructions.


7. Any meter may be used, but keep it simple.


  1. 8.The composition must be at the very least sixteen (16) measures in length (i.e., four, four measure phrases).


9. These compositions, when due, will be performed in class on that day. You and a partner will perform your works together. They shall be graded on the quality of performance as well as quality of composition.  Therefore, be sure to rehearse before you perform or have them performed.


10. Your finished composition should be fully edited, including tempo markings, phrasing and dynamics.


11. The instructor's copy must be in INK or a CLEAR computer printed or photocopy. It should include a COMPLETE analysis.


l2. No compositions shall be accepted late. This is a grade that CAN NOT be dropped.


HAPPY WRITING!!
